Homeless Encampments & Social Determinants of Health (San Diego)

End-to-end geospatial analysis examining relationships between homeless encampment locations and neighborhood-level socioeconomic indicators.
Tools: Python, GeoPandas, Census, ACS, TIGER/Line

Problem statement

How do neighborhood-level social and economic conditions relate to the spatial distribution of homeless encampments in San Diego?

Data sources

  • American Community Survey (5-year estimates)
  • Decennial Census / TIGER/Line boundary files
  • Homeless encampment point data (San Diego)

Geospatial workflow

  • Ingested ACS tabular data and TIGER/Line boundary geometries
  • Cleaned and normalized socioeconomic indicators
  • Performed spatial joins between encampment points and ZCTAs
  • Validated outputs (row counts, null checks, geometry consistency)
  • Prepared analysis-ready datasets for downstream use

Outputs

Analysis-ready datasets and static map outputs used to examine spatial patterns and inform subsequent exploratory and statistical analysis.

Pipeline alignment

This project represents an upstream geospatial ETL workflow that feeds downstream analysis and visualization projects (e.g., PITC × ACS correlations).
