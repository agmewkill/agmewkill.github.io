Encampments & Social Determinants of Health — Thesis Context

This page situates the encampments project within my thesis research on how tract-level social determinants of health (ACS proxies) relate to the spatial distribution of homelessness in San Diego County, using privacy-preserving, aggregated data.

Research framing

Core question: How do tract-level socioeconomic conditions correlate with the presence and intensity of homeless encampments over time?

  • Unit of analysis: Census tract
  • Outcome: Tract-aggregated encampment counts (PITC)
  • Explanatory variables: ACS 5-year indicators (SDoH proxies)
  • Intent: Exploratory, non-causal analysis

Cross-year correlation trends

Figure 1. Spearman correlations between tract-level PITC encampment counts and ACS indicators across multiple years.
Interpretation: While individual variables fluctuate year to year, several indicators maintain consistent directional relationships with encampment presence, suggesting structural rather than transient associations.

Top correlated ACS indicators

Figure 2. Heatmap of the strongest tract-level Spearman correlations between PITC counts and selected ACS variables.
Interpretation: Housing instability, poverty-related measures, and household structure indicators consistently rank among the strongest correlates, reinforcing their relevance for downstream modeling and policy analysis.
