PITC × ACS Correlations (San Diego County, 2013–2025)

This page documents the methods, assumptions, and analytical context for the PITC × ACS Correlations project. The interactive map and correlation panel are the second half of this same project.

Launch interactive Thesis context View code

What this project demonstrates

  • Multi-year data ingestion using year-configured CSV inputs and shared tract geometry
  • Schema normalization for real-world ACS column inconsistencies
  • Geospatial joins between tract-level PIT counts and demographic indicators
  • Inline QA and interpretability features (hover details, legends, correlation summaries)
  • Statistical computation of Spearman correlation (ρ) with safeguards for missing data

Data & assumptions

  • Inputs include PIT count totals by census tract and ACS 5-year tract-level indicators
  • Tract geometry is simplified for performance and interactive use
  • Some PIT years reuse the most recent available ACS vintage
  • Correlation values describe association only and do not imply causation

Correlation results

Spearman rank correlations between tract-level PITC counts and selected ACS indicators, evaluated across multiple years to assess stability and directionality.

Figure 1. Spearman correlations between PITC counts and all ACS variables across years. Lines illustrate the direction and relative stability of associations over time.
Figure 2. Heatmap of selected ACS indicators with the strongest Spearman correlations to PITC counts. Warmer colors indicate stronger positive association; cooler colors indicate negative association.

Interpretation (high-level): Across years, housing and poverty-adjacent indicators (e.g., renter prevalence, cash assistance, poverty measures) show more consistent associations with PITC patterns than many purely demographic indicators, though the strength and direction of relationships varies by year.
